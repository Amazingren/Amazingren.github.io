Talk 1

Remote Sensing Geo Foundation Models at Google

George Leifman

Google Research

Speaker Bio

George Leifman is a Senior Staff Software Engineer and Research Scientist at Google Research, where he leads initiatives in geospatial AI and foundation models. With over a decade of experience in computer vision and machine learning, he specializes in developing scalable solutions for remote sensing applications and multimodal data integration.

His current research focuses on building large-scale Geo Foundation Models that can process diverse geospatial data sources, contributing to Google's mission of organizing and making accessible the world's geographic information.

Abstract

At Google, our long-standing commitment to organizing and making accessible the world's information, exemplified by products like Google Maps and Earth, extends to harnessing the burgeoning wealth of remote sensing data. The explosion of sensors, from public sector programs like Sentinel and Landsat to high-resolution commercial satellites, presents both immense opportunity and significant challenges.

Our vision is to address these by developing large-scale, multimodal Geo Foundation Models (Geo FMs) that can learn rich representations from diverse data sources, including satellite imagery, weather information, associated texts, and eventually, human behavioral data. These pre-trained models serve as powerful starting points for a wide array of downstream applications, from urban planning to agriculture and forestry, enabling project-specific fine-tuning and optimization.

Talk 2

On the Path of Foundation Models for EO: Progress, Bottlenecks, and Possibilities

Alexandre Lacoste

ServiceNow Research

Speaker Bio

Alexandre is a Staff Research Manager leading the UI-Assist team at ServiceNow. Since his PhD in theoretical machine learning, he has published influential works on foundation models for EO, sequential decision-making, causality, and various works in collaboration with Yoshua Bengio. Prior to ServiceNow, he was the first research scientist to join Element AI and worked for three years at Google. He is also a founding member of ClimateChangeAI and an author of the seminal paper "Tackling Climate Change with Machine Learning."

Abstract

Foundation models are opening new possibilities for Earth Observation (EO), enabling tools that can support applications from agriculture to disaster response. As a community, we are building the foundations of this shift‚Äîbut also confronting clear bottlenecks.

This talk will map the current landscape: what today's EO benchmarks measure well, where blind spots remain, and which evaluations are still missing‚Äîsuch as temporality, multimodal sensor fusion, and robustness across regions. I will walk through the steps of developing and evaluating EO models, highlighting pain points we collectively face: limited access to permissively licensed (non-NC) imagery, missing metadata, scarce large-scale labeled datasets, and challenges in handling time and heterogeneous sensors.

Talk 3

Rethinking space science with physics-inspired foundation models

Jeroen Audenaert

MIT

Speaker Bio

Dr. Jeroen Audenaert is a Research Scientist at MIT, leading the development of machine learning and artificial intelligence techniques for NASA's Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS). He obtained his PhD from KU Leuven. Prior to joining MIT, he was the co-founder of the technology company Yellowleaf, which was acquired by an industry partner in the summer of 2023.

Abstract

Space science has entered the trillion-token data era, with missions like NASA's Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS) delivering millions of images and time-series each month. This unprecedented scale opens new frontiers, but also raises a key challenge: how can we automatically analyze such vast data streams in ways that remain interpretable and scientifically meaningful?

In this talk, I will discuss how physics-inspired causal foundation models offer a transformative path forward. I will present our latest work on building a time series foundation model for space science that is capable of generalizing across satellites by learning instrument-agnostic representations and explore how it can bring us into the era of automated, interpretible scientific discovery.

Talk 4

GeoLab: From Switzerland to Space and Back

Thomas Zurbuchen

ETH Zurich

Speaker Bio

Professor Thomas Zurbuchen is a Professor of Space Science and Policy at ETH Zurich and former Associate Administrator for NASA's Science Mission Directorate (2016-2022). During his tenure at NASA, he led the agency's science programs including the James Webb Space Telescope, Perseverance Mars rover, and Parker Solar Probe missions.

Before joining NASA, he was a professor at the University of Michigan for over two decades. He holds a PhD in Physics from the University of Bern and has authored over 200 scientific publications. At ETH, he focuses on developing innovative approaches to Earth observation and space science applications.

Abstract

Earth is changing rapidly, and our ability to respond depends on how well we turn vast, diverse streams of data into insight. At ETH Zurich, we are building the GeoLab as a hub dedicated to Switzerland's urgent need for scalable solutions to pressing challenges ‚Äî and to capturing the opportunities that arise from them.

Because Switzerland is small, diverse, and highly instrumented, it offers a unique calibration ground for global data sets and models. GeoLab is designed as an integration point: bringing together satellite observations, drone imagery, in-situ sensors, and advanced AI ‚Äî including foundation models ‚Äî into a coherent system that not only describes Earth, but also drives action in Switzerland and beyond.

Talk 5

A METEOR of SMARTIES

Devis Tuia

EPFL

Speaker Bio

Prof. Devis Tuia is a Professor at EPFL leading the Environmental Computational Science and Earth Observation Laboratory. His research focuses on machine learning for remote sensing and environmental monitoring, with particular expertise in deep learning, domain adaptation, and active learning for Earth observation applications.

He has authored over 150 scientific publications and received several awards including the IEEE GRSS Early Career Award. His current work emphasizes developing foundation models for environmental applications and physics-aware machine learning approaches for remote sensing data analysis.

Abstract

In this talk, I will present recent investigations toward foundation models for Earth observation, with a focus on agile downstream task adaptation with meta-learning and spectrum-aware self-supervised pre-training. By making models aware of the physics of data, it becomes possible to be competitive with much larger foundation models and tackle tasks with very limited labels.

Talk 6

Advancing Geospatial AI for Real-World Challenges

Ferda Ofli

Qatar Computing Research Institute (QCRI)

Speaker Bio

Dr. Ferda Ofli is a Principal Scientist at the Qatar Computing Research Institute (QCRI). His research lies at the intersection of computer vision, machine learning, and remote sensing, with a strong emphasis on applications for social good‚Äîparticularly in disaster response, crisis management, and sustainable development. He has authored more than 80 publications in top-tier venues and has received multiple awards including the AAAI Deployed Application Award (2024) and Outstanding Reviewer Awards from CVPR.

Abstract

The accelerating pace of disasters is testing the resilience of our societies and the capacity of humanitarian responders. At the same time, the explosion of multimodal data‚Äîfrom satellites and drones to social media and geospatial platforms‚Äîpresents both unprecedented opportunities and daunting challenges. AI has the potential to transform this raw data into actionable insights, yet today's models often fail when faced with such chaotic real-world conditions like noisy data inputs, distribution shifts, and incomplete information.

In this talk, I will present a vision for advancing geospatial AI that thrives in these high-stakes environments. Drawing from recent projects, I will share how we designed Flood Insights, a system that fuses satellite and social sensing data to map exposure, damage, and urgent needs during the 2022 Pakistan floods; how we benchmarked Large Language Models and Vision-Language Models to assess their robustness in handling disaster microblogs and scene understanding at unprecedented scale.

Talk 7

From Space to Safety with AI

Ioannis Papoutsis

National Technical University of Athens (NTUA)

Speaker Bio

Ioannis Papoutsis is an Assistant Professor of Remote Sensing and Artificial Intelligence at NTUA, and an Adjunct Researcher at both the National Observatory of Athens and the Archimedes/Athena Research Center. He leads the OrionLab research group, focusing on big satellite data analytics and machine learning for Earth Observation, with emphasis on natural disaster management and climate change impact monitoring.

Abstract

Advances in Earth Observation and Deep Learning are transforming the way we monitor, forecast, and manage natural disasters. This talk will explore recent progress in applying AI to critical hazards such as wildfires, floods, landslides, and volcanic unrest, highlighting the role of large-scale datasets, spatiotemporal datacubes, and emerging learning paradigms such as self-supervised and uncertainty-aware models.

By examining real-world applications and the challenges of data scarcity, class imbalance, and generalization across diverse environments, the talk will showcase how AI-driven methods can enhance disaster preparedness and response, while outlining key research directions for the future.

Talk 8

Towards Robust Visual Perception via Auxiliary Information and Tasks

Yimian Dai

Nankai University

Speaker Bio

Yimian Dai is currently an Associate Professor with the College of Computer Science, Nankai University. His research interests include computer vision, deep learning, and their applications in remote sensing, with a recent focus on infrared small target detection and multi-modal visual perception. He has published in leading journals and conferences such as IJCV, TGRS, and ICCV.

Abstract

Visual perception has recently achieved remarkable success in diverse tasks, including object detection, image segmentation, and image restoration. However, models trained on single-modality data or single-task objectives often fail to maintain reliable performance once deployed in complex real-world scenarios. These limitations arise not only from the incomplete feature extraction driven by a single task, but also from the inherent information loss within a single data modality.

To address these challenges, introducing auxiliary information and auxiliary tasks has become a promising strategy. By integrating additional modalities at the input stage, or by incorporating carefully designed auxiliary objectives through multitask learning, models can acquire more comprehensive and robust feature representations.

Talk 9

Agentic Reasoning Models for Earth Observation

Salman Khan

MBZUAI

Speaker Bio

Salman Khan is an Associate Professor at the MBZUAI, where he leads large-scale initiatives at the intersection of artificial intelligence, climate resilience, and Earth observation. He has co-authored over 200 papers in top AI venues, regularly serves as Area Chair at leading AI conferences. His work has been recognized with awards including Best Paper Award at ICML TerraBytes 2025.

Abstract

Earth Observation (EO) satellites are continuously capturing vast streams of multimodal data, offering an unprecedented view of our planet's dynamics at both global scale and fine resolution. Turning this raw information into actionable knowledge for addressing urgent challenges, such as climate extremes, disaster management, and food security, requires moving beyond pattern recognition toward AI systems that can reason, adapt, and interact with scientific tools.

In this talk, I will present recent advances in agentic AI for EO, highlighting how reasoning models can connect satellite observations with domain knowledge and physical simulators. This talk will introduce foundation models such as TerraFM and EarthDial, and show how simple agentic pipelines can help integrate multi-sensor imagery, reanalysis products, and hydrological or agricultural models to provide trustworthy and actionable insights.

Talk 11

Earth Observation with Large Multimodal Models

Danda Paudel

INSAIT

Speaker Bio

Dr. Danda Paudel is an Assistant Professor at INSAIT, Sofia University. His research focuses on computer vision, machine learning, and their applications to Earth observation and remote sensing. He has extensive experience in developing multimodal AI systems for environmental monitoring and geospatial analysis.

Before joining INSAIT, he worked as a research scientist in industry and academia, contributing to advances in foundation models and their applications to satellite imagery analysis. He is actively involved in the ELLIS network and collaborates internationally on projects related to AI for climate and Earth observation.

Abstract

I will present our ongoing work on the foundation model framework for Earth observation data from satellites and aerial sensors. By teaching the system to handle both spatial and temporal information, our model can now track changes over time‚Äîsuch as shifting landscapes, crop growth, or early signs of natural hazards.

This progress brings us closer to building a flexible co-pilot platform where people can interact with massive, complex datasets simply by asking questions in natural language. However, challenges remain: the diversity of sensor types, the enormous scale of data, and the need for strong performance under different conditions make the task difficult. Our results so far show great promise, paving the way for more accessible and powerful Earth observation tools.

Application scenarios like wildfire prediction and temporal data analysis will also be presented, along with the immediate challenges and opportunities in the domain.

From Efficient Foundation Models to AI Agents for Foundation Model Recommendations

Beg√ºm Demir

TU Berlin, BIFOLD

Speaker Bio

Prof. Beg√ºm Demir is a Professor at TU Berlin and the Head of the Remote Sensing Image Analysis group at BIFOLD. Her research focuses on developing advanced machine learning and signal processing algorithms for the analysis of multi-modal remote sensing data. She has authored over 150 scientific publications and received numerous awards including the IEEE GRSS Transactions Prize Paper Award.

Data acquired by satellites nowadays allow us to have a global view, consistent in time, of the state of our forests, oceans, and growing urban areas. However, such a wealth of data has little value without appropriate processing chains able to convert the pixel values to information useful for decision makers.

Recently, machine learning has seen fast advances‚Äîespecially thanks to the rise of deep learning methodologies‚Äîand is increasingly deployed in EO image processing systems. In this talk, our recent developments in the context of foundation models for EO will be presented.

GAIA 2025 Symposium Information

About GAIA 2025

The first GAIA Symposium on Geospatial AI and Applications with Foundation Models, hosted by INSAIT and ELLIS Unit Sofia, brings together leading researchers and practitioners in artificial intelligence, geospatial science, weather and climate, and Earth observation to explore the rapidly evolving intersection of machine learning, foundation models, and geospatial data.

The symposium aims to foster interdisciplinary collaboration, highlight recent scientific and technical advances, and catalyze the development of next-generation foundation models tailored for spatial and multimodal applications.

Key Topics

  • Multimodal foundation models for remote sensing
  • Self-supervised learning for Earth observation
  • Physics-informed AI for geospatial applications
  • Large-scale dataset development and benchmarking
  • Real-world deployment and robustness challenges
  • Applications in disaster response and climate science
